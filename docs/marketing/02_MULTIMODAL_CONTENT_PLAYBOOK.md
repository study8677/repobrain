# Multimodal Content Playbook

Status: Mandatory guide for campaign and asset production

## 1. Operating principle

The winning model is not video instead of text. The winning model is:

Video and visuals earn attention.  
Scannable text earns comprehension.  
Proof-rich depth earns trust and conversion.

## 2. Asset pairing table

| Situation | Attention asset | Validation asset | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| New offer launch | Short video, hero visual, LinkedIn post | Landing page + FAQ | Turn attention into evaluation |
| Cold prospecting | Email opener, short Loom/video, sharp problem statement | One-pager or use-case page | Prove relevance quickly |
| Website conversion | Hero section, product screenshot, process diagram | Proof block, FAQ, comparison table | Reduce decision friction |
| Social demand | Carousel, short clip, infographic | Article, landing page, case study | Capture and validate intent |
| Sales follow-up | Recap email, annotated screenshot | Proposal section, implementation plan | Turn interest into decision |
| Onboarding | Walkthrough video | Checklist + searchable guide | Increase adoption |
| Retention | Progress update visual | Performance summary + next actions | Reinforce value |
| Expansion | Use-case teaser | Role-specific recommendation or mini business case | Create new demand inside account |

## 3. Website and landing page standard

Every commercial page must contain:

1. Hero: who this is for and what outcome is promised.
2. Friction statement: the costly problem the buyer already feels.
3. Mechanism: how CorpFlowAI creates the result.
4. Visual proof: screenshot, workflow, diagram, before/after, or result structure.
5. Text proof: case, metric, testimonial, or operational evidence.
6. Fit criteria: who should and should not use it.
7. FAQ: objection handling.
8. CTA: one primary action and one lower-friction secondary action.

## 4. Visual direction rules

Use visuals when they do one of these jobs:

- Reduce abstraction.
- Show before and after.
- Clarify a workflow.
- Display proof.
- Make a choice easier.
- Compress complexity.
- Increase confidence.

Avoid visuals when they are decorative, generic, or disconnected from the buyer’s decision.

## 5. Video rules

Use video for:

- Explaining a workflow.
- Showing a product or process.
- Creating human trust.
- Demonstrating a transformation.
- Orienting new clients.
- Reducing support load.

Every video must have:

- A first 3-second hook.
- Clear on-screen text.
- A proof moment.
- A next step.
- A companion written asset.

## 6. Scannability rules

All written assets must use:

- Strong headings.
- Short paragraphs.
- Bullets only when they improve scan speed.
- Visible proof blocks.
- Comparison tables where choice is involved.
- Plain language.
- One idea per section.

## 7. Proof density by funnel stage

| Stage | Proof density | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| Awareness | Light | Use credibility cues and concrete mechanisms. Do not overload. |
| Consideration | Medium | Add examples, FAQs, comparison logic, and objections. |
| Conversion | High | Add implementation detail, risk reduction, pricing logic, and case proof. |
| Onboarding | Medium | Show process, next steps, examples, and success criteria. |
| Retention | High | Show outcomes, progress, adoption, and next opportunities. |

## 8. Content reuse model

Every campaign should produce a minimum set of reusable assets:

1. Hero claim.
2. Social post.
3. Short video script.
4. Landing page section.
5. FAQ block.
6. Sales follow-up email.
7. Objection response.
8. Visual brief.
9. Client/onboarding variant if the offer is sold.

No campaign should exist as only a post, only a video, or only a page.
