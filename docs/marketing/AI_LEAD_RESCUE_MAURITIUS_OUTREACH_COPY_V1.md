## Voice rules (apply to every channel)

1. **Lead with the named pain.** *"Most small businesses don't lose leads because they lack a website. They lose leads because enquiries arrive in different places and follow-up depends on memory."*
2. **Use buyer language, not internal language.** Buyer says *"missed an enquiry"*, *"didn't reply in time"*, *"the message got buried in WhatsApp"*. Buyer does NOT say *"lead rescue funnel orchestration"*.
3. **Specific numbers earn trust.** *USD 150*, *48 hours*, *7 days*, *2 business hours*, *one lead source*, *one named contact*. Avoid vague "fast", "quick", "soon".
4. **No "AI" first.** Lead with the operational outcome (capture / alert / log / daily summary). The mechanism includes AI-assisted tooling but that is not the headline.
5. **Single offer.** *AI Lead Rescue Setup - USD 150 launch pilot, invoiced after intake review.* Do not invent tiers or discounts.
6. **Single primary CTA per message.** Either *"Take a look at the page"* (link) or *"Want me to send you the page?"* (consent-led). Never both.
7. **No "buy now" / "pay now" / "instant".** Forbidden by `JE-2026-06-01-4` § 4.5.
8. **Honest pre-proof posture.** If asked for case studies, the truthful line is *"This is a launch pilot - we do not yet publish client cases. The first published case will appear after the first paying pilot completes - which is why the pilot price is USD 150 instead of the production price."*
9. **Mauritius identity is OK.** *"Built by a Mauritius-based operating-systems team"* is allowed (HQ fact, not a pricing claim). Do NOT say *"Trusted by Mauritian businesses"* (we don't have named clients yet).
10. **Plain language. No corporate filler.** *"Hope you're well"* / *"I trust this email finds you well"* are deletable. Lead with the named situation.

## 1. LinkedIn DM copy

### 1.1 Format and use

- 1:1 DM, sent **after** the LinkedIn connection request is accepted.
- ~3-5 short sentences, plain text, **no link in the first message** (LinkedIn deprioritises messages with external links in the first DM).
- Send the link in message 2 only after the prospect signals interest. This protects deliverability and reads less like a pitch.

### 1.2 Master copy (default for warm Mauritius prospect)

```text
Hi {first name}, thanks for connecting.

Mauritius say they sometimes lose enquiries simply because messages
arrive in too many places - WhatsApp, email, the website form,
Facebook - and follow-up depends on whoever remembers.

sources to a simple daily lead list, sends an instant alert to the
owner or operator, and surfaces the follow-ups due each morning.
USD 150 launch pilot, invoiced after we review your intake.

Want me to send you the one-page overview?
```

- Replace `{first name}` only - keep everything else verbatim until at least 10 sends, so we have a fair sample of replies to evaluate.

- If no reply within 5 working days: see § 4 follow-up message.

## 6. Objection-handling notes

These are reply-only - **do not** pre-empt these objections in the outreach itself. Pre-empting reads as defensive. Wait until the prospect raises the objection, then answer concisely.

### 6.1 *"USD 150 feels too cheap to be real."*

```text
That's a fair instinct. The USD 150 is deliberately the launch
pilot price - we're using the first cohort to publish proof
points so future buyers have named cases to look at. The
production price afterwards will be higher. The work itself is
the same 48-hour pilot either way.
```

### 6.2 *"USD 150 is a lot - can you do it cheaper?"*

```text
We've already set the launch pilot price as low as it goes
without giving up reliable delivery. Setup includes the lead
source connection, the alert, the daily list, the follow-up
board, and 7 days of monitoring. We don't discount further
because the work and the SLA don't change with the price.
```

### 6.3 *"Show me a case study / testimonial."*

```text
Honest answer: we don't yet publish client cases. This is a
launch pilot, and the first published case will appear after
the first paying pilot completes - which is exactly why the
price is USD 150 instead of the production price. The 52-second
walkthrough on the page shows the operator view with
representational data, clearly labelled, so you can see what the
system actually looks like before you decide anything.
```

### 6.4 *"Is this just AI? I don't trust AI for our customers."*

```text
The system uses some AI-assisted tooling under the hood, but the
buyer-facing outcome isn't AI - it's a connected lead source, an
instant alert to a human owner, a simple lead list, and a daily
follow-up summary. No AI talks to your customers, no AI sends
messages on your behalf. A human follows up; the system makes
sure the enquiry is in front of them.
```

### 6.5 *"How do I pay? Do you take cards?"*

```text
We don't collect card or banking details on the page. After we
review your intake (within 2 business hours), we email a USD 150
invoice with the agreed payment route. For Mauritius and
warm-network international, that's currently a SBM Bank Mauritius
wire. Once payment lands, the 48-hour setup clock starts.
```

### 6.6 *"I already use a CRM / spreadsheet / tool. I don't need this."*

```text
That's fine - Lead Rescue is intentionally not a CRM. It does
not replace your tools. It connects ONE existing lead source you
already use (form, email, WhatsApp, or Google Form) and makes
sure new enquiries are alerted, logged, and followed up. If your
current setup already does that reliably, you don't need us. If
a couple of enquiries a week still slip through, this is the
fix.
```

