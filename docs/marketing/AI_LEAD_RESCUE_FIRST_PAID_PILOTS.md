## 1. Objective — first 1–4 paid pilots

The objective for the first paid-pilot window is **proof, not volume**:

- **1 paid pilot** — proves the offer, the manual pro-forma path, and the 48-hour setup actually completes against a real owner.
- **2–4 paid pilots** — proves the workflow is repeatable across more than one buyer, generates the first published case material (with consent), and stress-tests the operator cockpit + activity log.

We deliberately stop at 4. A fifth paying client before the first four are running cleanly is a forecasting win that becomes an operational debt — operator capacity, monitoring discipline, and case material all degrade if pilots run faster than they can be reviewed.

The whole window is **structurally pre-proof**: there are no published clients yet, no testimonials, no logos, no "Trusted by" claims. The pricing reflects that — see § 7.

## 2. Niches

### 2.1 First niche — Mauritius property / real-estate operators

Why first:

- **Highest enquiry volume per business.** A single rental agent typically receives enquiries through Property24, Lemons, WhatsApp, Facebook DMs, the agency website, and walk-ins.
- **Multi-channel triage today is memory-based.** Agents lose viewings to whoever calls back first. The "missed enquiry" pain is real and immediate.
- **Daily-summary outcome maps cleanly to "viewings booked vs viewings missed last week"** — the buyer can verify the value with one Monday-morning view.
- **Warm network density is high.** Mauritius is a small market; one decent introduction reaches several agents.
- **No regulated-data complications.** Property enquiries are not medical, financial, or legal records.

### 2.2 Secondary niches

- **Contractors / trades / home services / renovation / solar / security.** High-value quotes (MUR 50k+); team is on site all day; WhatsApp + email + Facebook are the primary channels; slow response loses to whoever calls back first.
- **Clinics / wellness / dental / beauty / admin practices.** Weekend enquiry pile-ups; appointment requests on WhatsApp + Facebook DMs + website forms; no Monday-morning view today. **Important:** the offer is appointment-enquiry follow-up only, not medical advice or clinical triage (per `docs/strategy/ABOVE_THE_LINE_STRATEGY_DOCTRINE.md` § *Done-for-you business function model*). Do not engage with clinical-decision-making scope.

### 2.3 Why Mauritius first

- **Operator presence.** Anton is on the ground; same time zone, same WhatsApp behaviour, same payment expectations.
- **Warm network proximity.** Real referrals are available; cold prospecting is not required for the first 4 pilots.
- **Manual pro-forma path works locally.** Mauritius wire transfer (SBM) is the agreed payment route for warm-network buyers; no card processor is required for the launch pilot.
- **Above-the-line fit.** The offer can be sold as a *managed lead-response operating workflow with a human operator* — not a generic AI tool — because Anton is the operator.

## 3. Who to target

### 3.1 Strong-fit buyer profile

- Owner-operator or operations-lead at a small business (1–20 staff) in one of the niches in § 2.
- Receives enquiries through **at least two** channels (WhatsApp + Facebook + website form + email is typical) and personally handles follow-up.
- Has explicitly mentioned, in conversation or on social, that **enquiries get lost** / *"didn't reply in time"* / *"the message got buried in WhatsApp"* — buyer-language pain, not internal CRM language.
- Already has a website and a working email address (we are not selling website services).
- Has the authority to approve a USD 150 invoice without a procurement cycle.
- Is comfortable with a **48-hour pilot setup** and a **7-day monitoring window** before any decision about monthly monitoring.

### 3.2 Who NOT to target in the first paid-pilot window

- **Cold international prospects.** Out of scope per `JE-2026-06-01-4` § 4.4 until the warm-network pilot batch produces 1 published case.
- **Prospects without a real referral or warm path.** Cold-scraped contact lists are forbidden for v1 — this stays a relationship-based wedge.
- **Enterprise prospects with procurement cycles.** Schedule a separate `LR-Mauritius-Enterprise-Track-1` packet later.
- **Regulated-data verticals that require a signed DPA before any conversation** (regulated finance, healthcare records, legal). Approach only after the standard `docs/operations/SECURITY_REVIEW_CHECKLIST.md` flow.
- **Anyone who wants a "full CRM build", "AI agent for our team", "chatbot on our website that closes deals", or "guaranteed lead volume".** Decline the misframed offer; refer to the doctrine line in § 9.
- **Anyone who already has a working follow-up board they are happy with.** Forcing the offer onto a buyer who does not feel the pain is the fastest path to a refund request.

### 3.3 Buyer-pain tells worth acting on

When you hear any of these in conversation, that is a green light to send the § 4 outreach:

| Pain phrase | Niche signal |
|---|---|
| *"viewing requests probably slipped through last week"* | Property |
| *"appointment requests probably arrived over the weekend"* | Clinic / wellness |
| *"a quote went cold because we didn't reply in time"* | Contractor / trades |
| *"the message got buried in WhatsApp"* | Cross-vertical |
| *"my admin is on leave and enquiries pile up"* | Owner-managed practice |
| *"I have no idea how many we missed"* | Cross-vertical (strongest signal) |

## 7. Discovery call flow summary

The full script lives in **`docs/sales/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_DISCOVERY_CALL_SCRIPT.md`** (this PR). 15-minute structure:

1. **Opening (1 min).** Frame the call as a working session; restate why they replied; ask for permission to ask 5 short questions.
2. **Pain questions (3 min).** Where do enquiries arrive today? Who handles them? What gets missed?
3. **Current lead-flow questions (3 min).** What channels, what response time today, what tooling.
4. **Response-time + follow-up questions (3 min).** What is the cost to them when an enquiry is missed.
5. **Qualification questions (2 min).** Owner authority for the USD 150 invoice. 48-hour setup willingness.
6. **Close for paid pilot (2 min).** *"Based on what you've told me, the pilot fits — would you like me to send the intake link so we can start the 48-hour setup?"*
7. **Payment / manual invoice next step (1 min).** Send the intake link; confirm timing for the invoice; explain the manual pro-forma route.

If the prospect is not ready: thank them, offer to send the page anyway as a no-pressure follow-up, propose a check-in in 2–4 weeks. **No high-pressure close.** This is a wedge offer; we want willing first pilots, not coerced ones.

## 9. Objection handling summary

The full library lives in **`docs/sales/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_OUTREACH_SCRIPTS.md`** § *Objection handling*. Doctrine answers (one-line summaries):

- **"Send me details"** → send the page link with one sentence framing what they will see.
- **"How much?"** → USD 150 launch pilot; everything is on the page; invoiced after intake review.
- **"We already use WhatsApp"** → great — the pilot connects WhatsApp + the other channels into one Monday-morning view; we do not replace WhatsApp.
- **"We already have a website"** → we are not selling website services; we connect the enquiries the website already produces into a daily lead list.

If the conversation drifts toward "build us a chatbot", "build us an AI agent", "guarantee us X new leads per month", or "replace our sales team", the doctrine line is:

> *"This is a managed lead-response operating workflow with a human operator. It is not a chatbot, an AI agent, or a revenue guarantee. If those are what you need, this is not the right offer for you."*

Decline politely and move on.

## 10. Pilot success criteria

A pilot is **successful** when **all** of the following are true at the end of the 7-day monitoring window:

| # | Success criterion | Evidence on the operator cockpit |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Buyer's enquiries are landing in the agreed Google Sheet | Setup checklist item 8 (`lead_appears_in_sheet`) = `done` |
| 2 | Buyer is receiving the Telegram / owner alert per new enquiry | Setup checklist item 7 (`alert_received`) = `done` |
| 3 | Daily summary is reaching the buyer at the agreed time / channel | Setup checklist item 10 (`daily_summary_configured`) = `done` |
| 4 | Buyer has used the daily summary at least **once** to act on a follow-up | Activity log entry of type `prospect_replied` or `note` referencing buyer behaviour |
| 5 | Zero unrecoverable data loss during the 7 days | No incident-shaped activity log entries |
| 6 | Buyer has read the hand-over message and knows who to contact | Setup checklist item 11 (`client_handover_sent`) = `done` |

A pilot that passes these criteria becomes a candidate for **monthly monitoring** (`MONITORING_OFFERED → MONTHLY_ACTIVE`). A pilot that does not pass them is **closed honestly**: the operator emails the buyer a one-paragraph summary, refunds proportionally if scope was not delivered, and records `bad_fit` or `LOST` in the cockpit. We do not roll a failed pilot into monthly monitoring.

## 11. Handoff from sales to operator

Anton is currently both — but the handoff is documented so the workflow scales when a second operator joins:

1. **Sales side closes** — the buyer submits intake on `/lead-rescue`; the manual pro-forma is sent; payment lands.
2. **Status moves to `PAID_SETUP`** — see `docs/operations/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_OPERATOR_RUNBOOK.md` § *From paid pilot to setup* (added in this PR).
3. **The 13-item setup checklist appears on `/admin/lead-rescue/[id]`** — operator works through it within 48 hours.
4. **Activity log is used to keep the timeline honest** — `intake_reviewed`, `delivery_handoff`, follow-up scheduling, prospect replies. See § *How to use the activity log* in the runbook.
5. **Monitoring window opens (`LIVE_PILOT`)** — operator watches the Telegram alerts + Sheet daily for 7 days.
6. **Monthly monitoring offered (`MONITORING_OFFERED`)** — operator-decided based on § 10 success criteria.

## 12. What to do after payment

Operator-side checklist (canonical version in `docs/operations/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_PAID_PILOT_ONBOARDING.md`):

- Confirm payment landed in the agreed account (do not start the 48-hour clock until the wire has cleared, not just been sent).
- Reply to the buyer in the same channel they replied on (WhatsApp / email / LinkedIn) within **2 business hours** confirming receipt and the 48-hour window starting time.
- Move the lead to `PAID_SETUP` on `/admin/lead-rescue/[id]`.
- Open the 13-item setup checklist and start with item 1 (`intake_reviewed`).
- Add an activity-log entry of type `payment_confirmed_manual` with channel `manual` and a one-line note.
- Schedule a **midpoint check-in** at hour 24 (operator-side calendar) to confirm checklist progress is on track.
- Schedule a **handover message** at hour 46 (operator-side calendar) to draft the buyer hand-over note before the 48-hour mark.

## 14. How the activity log fits the paid-pilot workflow

> **Status note:** the activity-log runtime feature is being shipped in PR #331 (`feat(lead-rescue): add outbound activity log`). This pack assumes the feature **may not yet be live in production** when an operator first reads it. If the feature is live, use the workflow below. If the feature is not live yet, treat this section as **planned — do not depend on it** and use the existing notes / `next_action` fields on the operator cockpit until the runtime PR is merged + live-verified per `.cursor/rules/delivery-reality.mdc`.

When live, the activity log replaces ad-hoc notes for outreach and follow-up tracking. Every paid-pilot conversation should produce a sequence like:

1. **Outbound opener sent** — `outbound_opener` on the channel used; one-line note (the one-line message body, no PII beyond first name + business).
2. **Prospect replied** — `prospect_replied`; one-line note summarising the gist of the reply (no full message thread; no card / banking / password / health details).
3. **Discovery call booked** — `call_booked`; `next_action_date` set to the scheduled time.
4. **Discovery call completed** — `note`; one-line summary of qualification answers § 8.
5. **Manual pro-forma sent** — `manual_pro_forma_sent`; channel `email`; reference to the manual pro-forma fallback doc.
6. **Payment confirmed** — `payment_confirmed_manual`; channel `manual`; the 48-hour clock starts.
7. **Delivery handoff** — `delivery_handoff` once the lead reaches `PAID_SETUP`; the 13-item setup checklist takes over for delivery.
8. **Bad-fit / declined** if the conversation closes without a paid pilot — `bad_fit`; one-line note explaining why so we learn from each pass.

The activity log is **append-only** and **server-stamps** the timestamp + operator label. Do not paste card numbers, banking credentials, passwords, OTPs, or private health details into any field — see `docs/operations/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_OPERATOR_RUNBOOK.md` § *What not to store*.
