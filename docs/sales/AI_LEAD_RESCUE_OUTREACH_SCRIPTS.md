## How to use this document

- These scripts are paste-ready. Edit the `{first name}`, `{business name}`, and `{specific recent missed-enquiry pain phrase}` placeholders before sending. Do **not** send any script without that personalisation.
- One primary CTA per message. Either *"Take a look at the page"* (link) or *"Want me to send you the page?"* (consent-led). Never both.
- No "AI" in the hook. Lead with the operational outcome.
- No bulk send. Each message is sent to one named person Anton has a real reason to reach out to.
- LinkedIn DMs: no link in the first message. Send the link in message 2 only after the prospect signals interest.
- Forbidden phrases (per `JE-2026-06-01-4` § 4.5 + brand doctrine): *buy now, pay now, instant, guaranteed, fully autonomous, never miss a lead again, replace your team, 10x your business*.

### Mauritius property variant — use the property-specific blocks

The blocks in this file target the **apex `/lead-rescue`** page (pan-vertical, USD-anchor invoicing). For prospects in **Mauritius property** (real estate agencies, villa rental operators, property managers, serviced-apartment / short-term rentals), use the paste-ready blocks in **`docs/marketing/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_MAURITIUS_OUTREACH_COPY_V1.md` § 7.1.1** instead.

Why a separate set:

- The property-Mauritius blocks link to **`https://corpflowai.com/lead-rescue/property-mauritius`** (not the apex), because the property page has a property-specific operator view, segment-fit copy, and a different payment trust block.
- The property-Mauritius blocks use the **local pro-forma framing** the property page itself uses: *"invoiced as the MUR equivalent of USD 150 on a local pro-forma, bank transfer to a Mauritius bank account, proof of payment shared manually before setup."* This localisation is permitted **only on the Mauritius property surface** per `docs/marketing/BRAND_AND_CONVERSION_DOCTRINE.md` § *Mauritius property page localisation note* — the apex `/lead-rescue` and all other surfaces continue to use the USD-invoice framing in this file.
- Continuation pricing (USD 99 / month operator-side per `docs/sales/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_PRICING_GUIDE.md` § 1) is **never mentioned in first-touch outreach** on either surface; it is quoted only after the 7-day pilot if the buyer opts in.

## 1. WhatsApp script (1:1, warm contact)

### 1.1 Opener (first message after a real reason to reach out)

```text
Hi {first name} — quick reason I'm messaging.

Most small businesses I speak to in Mauritius say they sometimes lose
enquiries simply because messages arrive in too many places (WhatsApp,
website, Facebook, email) and follow-up depends on whoever remembers.

We've put together a 48-hour pilot that connects one of those lead
sources to a daily lead list, sends an instant alert to the owner,
and surfaces the follow-ups due each morning. USD 150 launch pilot,
invoiced after we review your intake.

Want me to send you the one-page overview?
```

### 1.2 Reply if they say "yes, send it"

```text
Here you go: https://corpflowai.com/lead-rescue

Page is the full offer — what gets set up, what we don't promise
(we don't guarantee revenue, only that existing enquiries become
visible and trackable), and a short intake form at the bottom.

If it fits, fill the intake and I'll come back within ~2 business
hours with the invoice.
```

### 1.3 Follow-up after no reply (3–4 days later, only if appropriate)

```text
Hi {first name}, no pressure if the timing isn't right — just wanted
to leave the page in case it's useful: https://corpflowai.com/lead-rescue

If it's not for you, a one-line "not for us" is genuinely useful so
I don't keep nudging.
```

## 2. Email script (warm contact, plain text)

### 2.1 Subject line options (pick one based on relationship)

- *Quick thought re missed enquiries at {business name}*
- *USD 150 lead-rescue pilot — does this fit {business name}?*
- *Following up: lead-rescue pilot for {business name}*

### 2.2 Body

```text
Hi {first name},

I'm working on a small productized offer for owner-managed businesses
in Mauritius and I think it might fit how {business name} runs day-to-day.

The short version:

Most owners I speak to say they sometimes lose enquiries because
messages arrive in too many places — WhatsApp, the website form,
Facebook, email — and follow-up depends on memory. We've packaged a
48-hour pilot that:

- Connects one of those lead sources to a daily lead list (Google Sheet).
- Sends an instant alert to the owner / operator on every new enquiry.
- Surfaces the follow-ups due each morning.
- Runs a 7-day monitoring window so we can see what's actually moving.

USD 150 launch pilot, invoiced after we review your intake. No card
on the page; we send the invoice through the agreed route once the
scope is confirmed. We do not guarantee new revenue — we help make
sure existing enquiries are captured, visible, and followed up.

Page: https://corpflowai.com/lead-rescue

If it looks like a fit, the intake form at the bottom is the
fastest path. Happy to answer questions before you fill anything in.

Best,
Anton
```

## 7. Objection handling (one-line answers + extended scripts)

Use the one-line first; extend only if the prospect pushes back.

### 7.1 *"Send me details"*

**One-line:** *"Page is here, full offer + intake form at the bottom: https://corpflowai.com/lead-rescue"*

**Extended (only if asked):**

```text
The page is the canonical version — every commitment is there
including what we don't promise. If you have a specific question
the page doesn't answer, ask me directly and I'll tell you straight.
```

### 7.2 *"How much?"*

**One-line (apex `/lead-rescue`):** *"USD 150 launch pilot — invoiced after we review your intake. Everything is on the page."*

**One-line (Mauritius property `/lead-rescue/property-mauritius`):** *"MUR equivalent of USD 150 on a local pro-forma — bank transfer to a Mauritius bank account, no card on the page. Setup starts after manual confirmation."*

**Extended (only if asked, apex):**

```text
USD 150, one-time, for the 48-hour pilot setup — that includes one
lead-source connection, owner alerts, the daily lead list, and 7
days of monitoring after we go live. If you want to continue
monitoring after the pilot we'll discuss it then; nothing is
auto-renewed.
```

**Extended (only if asked, Mauritius property):**

```text
The pilot is invoiced as the MUR equivalent of USD 150 on a local
pro-forma — the final MUR amount is confirmed on the invoice itself
at the day's rate. You pay by bank transfer to a Mauritius bank
account and share the proof of payment manually. The 48-hour setup
begins only after we manually confirm the wire has cleared. No card
on the page, no online checkout, no automated subscription. If you
want to continue monitoring after the 7-day pilot, we discuss it
then; nothing on the page commits you to that.
```

### 7.3 *"We already use WhatsApp"*

**One-line:** *"Great — the pilot connects WhatsApp + your other channels into one Monday-morning view; we don't replace WhatsApp."*

**Extended (only if asked):**

```text
The pilot's job is to take whatever lead sources you already use —
WhatsApp included — and produce a single daily lead list and an alert
per new enquiry. WhatsApp is not replaced; it's surfaced. If you
already have a process that's working, we don't try to change it.
```

### 7.4 *"We already have a website"*

**One-line:** *"We're not selling website services — we connect the enquiries the website already produces into a daily lead list."*

**Extended (only if asked):**

```text
The pilot doesn't touch your website. We take whatever enquiries it
already produces (the form, the email it sends, the WhatsApp button)
and route them into a daily lead list with an alert per new
enquiry. If your website is fine, perfect — we work with what's there.
```

### 7.5 *"We do not want a CRM"*

**One-line:** *"This is not a CRM — it's a lead-response operating workflow. No data migration, no contacts database, no licences."*

**Extended (only if asked):**

```text
We deliberately don't sell a CRM. The pilot is a managed
lead-response workflow — a daily lead list (Google Sheet), an alert
per new enquiry (Telegram or email), and a follow-up board with
five statuses. No contact database, no migrations, no per-seat
licences. If you ever need a real CRM later, this won't lock you in.
```

### 7.6 *"Is this AI spam?"*

**One-line:** *"Fair question — the operator personally reviews every intake; AI assists with capture and summary, follow-up decisions are human."*

**Extended (only if asked):**

```text
Honest answer: AI tools are used internally to keep the workflow
fast (capturing the enquiry, drafting the daily summary). But the
person you're talking to (me) personally reviews intake and signs off
on the setup. Nothing is auto-sent to your customers without operator
review. The page lists exactly what gets set up.
```

### 7.7 *"Can you guarantee X new leads / Y% more conversion?"*

**One-line:** *"No — we never guarantee revenue. The page says so explicitly. The pilot is for owners who want existing enquiries to stop slipping through, not for owners who need a revenue-guarantee product."*

This answer is intentionally hard. If the prospect needs a revenue guarantee, they are not a fit. Decline politely.

### 7.8 *"Can you build us a chatbot / AI agent?"*

**One-line:** *"This isn't that. AI Lead Rescue is a managed lead-response operating workflow. If you specifically need a chatbot, this isn't the right offer."*

This answer is also intentionally hard — see the doctrine line in `docs/marketing/AI_LEAD_RESCUE_FIRST_PAID_PILOTS.md` § 9. Decline politely and move on.

## 8. What NOT to write (forbidden vocabulary)

| Avoid | Why | Use instead |
|---|---|---|
| *"buy now"* / *"pay now"* / *"instant"* | Forbidden by `JE-2026-06-01-4` § 4.5 | *"invoiced after we review your intake"* |
| *"guaranteed"* / *"never miss a lead again"* | Brand doctrine forbids unsupported claims | *"helps make follow-up visible"* |
| *"fully autonomous"* / *"replace your team"* | Above-the-line doctrine forbids generic-AI framing | *"managed lead-response workflow with a human operator"* |
| *"10x your business"* / *"revolutionary"* / *"game-changing"* | Brand doctrine — generic AI cadence | Specific outcome (capture / alert / log / daily summary) |
| *"unlimited leads"* / *"unlimited messages"* | Implies revenue guarantee | Concrete numbers (USD 150 / 48 h / 7 days / 2 business hours) |
| *"AI agent for your business"* | Generic positioning; below the line | *"managed lead-response operating workflow"* |
| *"Trusted by Mauritian businesses"* | Pre-proof window — we have no published clients yet | *"Built by a Mauritius-based operating-systems team"* (HQ fact) |
